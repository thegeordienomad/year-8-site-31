Horse Riding

Sandra's persuasive writing essay taken from the winter progression test

28/12/2021

What is horse-riding? It’s a sport that involves communicating with a 600kg animal, gaining it’s trust and establishing friendship. Horses have been a form of transport for a long time, although they were only recently introduced into the Olympics. There are different variations of this sport (e.g., dressage, show-jumping, reining and racing) that you can participate in. Some prefer dressage. Others prefer jumping. So there is something for everyone.

The way I see it, the sport is amazing as you have the rare opportunity to non-verbally communicate with the animal using just your hands and legs. Every equestrian has their own “hear-horse”. A horse that helped them the most through their journey. A horse they will never forget.

You have to push beyond your limits to achieve a strong friendship and trust. Once you have achieved that you will have a lifetime best friend.

If you ever decide to try you should get a professional instructor that will gradually teach you the basics and allow you to become a skilled rider. You should always remain calm and be quick thinking. A horse can feel if you are nervous or uncertain and will match these feelings. If you are about to jump a barrier you should keep the same pace; go faster or calm the horse down. You only have a couple of seconds to decide as the horse must understand your commands with enough time to react.

Overall, the most important thing is to enjoy yourself and do what you love.
